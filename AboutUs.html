Who is 'Q The Mic'?

Place Mouse Here

We are a radio show/podcast that is LGBTQIA-centered, with topics for everyone! From the ourtrageous to the outrageously funny, this show is about our humanity and the threads (no matter how tenuous) that connect us. Tune in every week for our Why Pronouns Matter segment, Fox's Rant of The Week, Gay Answers to Straight Questions (GASQ), and Newsworthy or Not! We welcome guests from all walks of life, and have been graced with so many wonderful
guests.

Dr. Renee Norris-Jones

Producer and Host
She/Her

About Renee

Seasoned radio host/producer (92.9 WGGT & 106.5 WPPM). award-winning television producer/co-host for Tapestry of Life on CCP-TV Comcast 53, FIOS 21, and in classrooms at Community College of Philadelphia and Temple University. A serial entrepreneur, Renee's social platform is Domestic Violence. She sits on the board at Women Against Abuse. Renee loves opera, nature, the stage (Broadway of course) and exercising her green thumb.

Griffin Fadellin

Co-Host
They/Them

About Griffin

Griffin Fadellin is a Liberal Arts major at the Community College of Philadelphia, and a massive fan of making strangers uncertain of their gender. As the Managing Editor of Print for the Vanguard, CCP’s student newspaper, they enjoy writing about arts, politics, and LGBT+ issues. They adore Philly, their home city, but just might love learning arcane, nerdy factoids a bit more. Their passions include tabletop gaming, long walks to get coffee, and making the world a better place.

Jamie Lyn Sawczyszyn AKA Fox

Co-Host and Assistant Producer
She/Her

About Fox

Originally from New York, Fox is a writer, slam poet, and singer. With a long list of passion projects that remain half-finished, she is currently learning how to tell computers what to do (computer programming). She has written pieces for Feminine Collective, and plans to publish a book of slam poetry in the future.

About Sandy

If you're looking to get attention via the written word, he's the person to turn to. He's launched and edited newspapers that have won a combined seven awards for excellence from his peers in academic public relations. In addition to his full-time position at PhillyMag and his ongoing sideline at Next City, his work as a writer has been published in The Philadelphia Inquirer, the Philadelphia CityPaper, CityMayors.com, and other print and online media.
